Career Guessing Mode
Inspired by Max Klymenko's Career Ladder interviews. Uses K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN, k=3) trained on a custom career dataset with 15 features including whether the work is creative, technical, people-facing, or location-specific. The model finds the three most similar real career profiles to your answers and reveals the top match. Euclidean distance is used to measure similarity.
KNN (k=3) Euclidean distance OrdinalEncoder career_ladder.csv 15 features
🧭
Career Exploration — Model 1 (Real dataset, Around 50% accuracy)
Uses AdaBoost trained on three merged datasets where career labels come directly from the real Recommended_Career column, simplified into 6 categories. Big5 personality traits and study habits are randomly sampled from their respective datasets. This creates a real-world disconnection between features and labels — which is why accuracy is ~50%. This model is honest about uncertainty.
AdaBoost Real dataset labels ~50% accuracy 3 datasets merged Honest uncertainty
⭐
Career Exploration — Model 2 (Profile-based, around 99% accuracy)
Also uses AdaBoost but with personality and habit profiles assigned per career category. Rule based is added which results in much higher accuracy but it trades off some real-world randomness for learnable structure.
AdaBoost Profile-based training ~80% accuracy Rule-based structure

Datasets Used
This project combines multiple datasets for career prediction and exploration.
AI-based Career Recommendation System
Skills, interests, and recommended careers — used for interest keyword features and career labels.
Big Five Personality Dataset (data-final.csv)
50 personality items scored 1–5, averaged into 5 trait scores (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism), normalized to 0–1.
Enhanced Student Habits & Performance
Study hours, attendance, motivation, stress, learning style — normalized with MinMaxScaler.
Career Ladder (career_ladder.csv)
Custom manually curated dataset of diverse careers with 15 categorical and numerical features, inspired by Max Klymenko's career format.
